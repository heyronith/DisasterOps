# Example Incident Reports for DisasterOps

Copy and paste any of these examples into the Incident Report field in the Streamlit app.

---

## Example 1: Wildfire Evacuation

```
Wildfire approaching residential area. 200 homes at risk in Pine Valley subdivision. Winds 25mph from the west, dry conditions, low humidity. Fire is approximately 2 miles away and moving east. 5 firefighters on scene, 2 fire engines available. Evacuation ordered for zones 3-5. Need immediate evacuation plan, resource requests for additional fire crews, and emergency shelter coordination. Road closures on Highway 47. Multiple residents reporting smoke inhalation symptoms.
```

---

## Example 2: Flash Flood Response

```
Flash flooding in downtown area after heavy rainfall. Multiple vehicles stranded on Main Street and 5th Avenue. Water level 3 feet in some areas, rising rapidly. 12 people rescued from vehicles so far. 3 reported injuries: 1 with possible broken leg, 2 with minor cuts and bruises. Roads impassable due to debris and water. Water receding slowly but more rain expected in next 2 hours. Power out in 3 blocks. CERT teams mobilizing. Need medical resources, evacuation assistance, and structural damage assessment.
```

---

## Example 3: Earthquake with Multiple Injuries

```
5.8 magnitude earthquake struck central district at 2:45 PM. Multiple buildings damaged. 50+ people need medical attention. Power outages in affected areas. Communications disrupted. Several multi-story buildings with visible structural damage. Search and rescue operations needed. CERT teams mobilizing. 3 ambulances on scene, hospital requesting additional medical personnel. Aftershocks continuing. Need structural engineers, search and rescue teams, medical resources, and temporary shelter coordination.
```

---

## Example 4: Hurricane Preparedness

```
Hurricane approaching coastal area. Expected landfall in 48 hours. High winds and storm surge expected. Mandatory evacuation ordered for coastal zones 1-3. Emergency shelters opening at 3 locations. 500 residents need evacuation assistance. Limited transportation resources available. Need evacuation coordination, shelter management, resource allocation for food and water, and communication plans for residents. Emergency Operations Center activated.
```

---

## Example 5: Chemical Spill - Industrial

```
Chemical spill at industrial facility on Industrial Parkway. Unknown chemical composition. Hazmat team responding. Downwind area evacuated within 1 mile radius. 200 residents evacuated. Multiple responders on scene including fire department and hazmat specialists. Need chemical identification, decontamination protocols, medical monitoring for exposed individuals, and coordination with environmental agencies. Weather conditions: light wind from southwest.
```

---

## Example 6: Tornado Damage

```
Tornado touched down in residential area. Multiple homes destroyed. Power lines down. Gas leaks reported. 15 people injured, 2 critical. 3 fatalities confirmed. Search and rescue teams deployed. Hospital requesting additional medical supplies. Roads blocked by debris. Need immediate search and rescue resources, medical personnel, structural damage assessment, and utility company coordination for gas leaks and power restoration.
```

---

## Example 7: Winter Storm - Power Outage

```
Severe winter storm with ice accumulation. Widespread power outages affecting 5,000 residents. Temperatures dropping below freezing. Multiple trees down on roads. 2 warming centers open but at capacity. Need additional shelter space, generator resources, road clearing equipment, and communication plan for residents about safety and shelter locations. Emergency management requesting resource assistance.
```

---

## Example 8: Mass Casualty Incident - Building Collapse

```
Multi-story building collapse in downtown area. Multiple casualties trapped in rubble. Fire department on scene with search and rescue operations. 25 people rescued so far, 10 with serious injuries. Unknown number still trapped. Heavy equipment needed for debris removal. Medical staging area established. Need additional search and rescue teams, medical personnel, heavy equipment, and coordination with structural engineers for safe rescue operations.
```

## Quick Copy: Wildfire Example

Wildfire approaching residential area. 200 homes at risk. Winds 25mph, dry conditions. 5 firefighters on scene. Need evacuation plan and resource requests.
